How to use this webpage effectively
===================================

You will see that my projects are a blend of theoretical research, algorithm development, software implementation,
optimization and more. I know that it might be difficult to find what you are looking for, espacially if
you know what you need, but you don't know how to find it. Don't worry, I respect yout time and I tried to
make the hiring process as smooth as possible. Here are a few tips to help you navigate through my portfolio:

* If you are looking for a specific skillset, framework or tool, use the search bar to find relevant projects.

* If you are looking for a specific role, eg. data scientist, optimization engineer etc., check out the 
  :ref:`Roles` section.

* If you get lost, you can always return to the homepage by clicking on the "Home" link in the navigation bar.

The structure of this website
=============================

- :ref:`About`: Get to know me better, my educational background, and my journey as a professional. I suggest you to start
  here to get a better understanding of who I am and what I can offer.

- :ref:`Projects`: Explore my projects, research, and software development work. Each project is unique and showcases a
  different aspect of my skills and expertise.

- :ref:`Roles`: Learn about why and how I can be categorized as a data scientist, software developer, structural engineer, optimization
  engineer, technical writer or researcher. This section helps you to make the connection between my skills and the specific role
  you are looking for.

- :ref:`Studies`: Here I present studies that are not connected to projects, but are investigations I made on specific topics.

- :ref:`Research`: A summary of my research history, including a list of my most important publications: 
  conference papers, journal articles, and other scientific works.

- :ref:`Contact`: Here you find all the info to contact me.
